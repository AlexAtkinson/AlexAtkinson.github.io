My Priorities

Simply put: Quality of Life -- everything else comes naturally if this is your north star.

Simple to say, and difficult to achieve, but keep the following in mind:

  • The happy employee produces better product, and more meaningfully participates in the organization.
  • Healthier organizations have happier customers.
  • Happy customers result in higher NPS.
  • Measure both NPS and eNPS regularly -- they are intrinsically linked.
  • Do 360 reviews. It doesn't take much for an organization to slide.
  • Have a culture of continuous improvement.
  • Practice servant leadership -- even if you're not yet leadership.
  • Be familiar with The Toyota Way (LEAN), SAFe/Agile, and other relevant methodologies, but understand that none of it is doctrine.
  • Know the 12-factors.
  • Don't over-value metrics. Be wary of their misuse and always consider the context.

These are just a few, high-level, leadership-targeted points.
Above all, remember that people are your greatest asset. Technology is just a tool to help us all achieve more, and ultimately improve our circumstances in life.
